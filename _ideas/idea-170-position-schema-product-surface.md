## Idea

Position schema as its own product surface. The position schema, once developed as methodology infrastructure (Tier 2), has potential to become its own surface product within the Ostr-itch or Polity ecosystem. A person could occupy a position (or have it attributed to them) and use the position surface to:

(a) determine whether to hire someone for the position,
(b) define expectations for the position,
(c) discover tools and dependencies the position should have,
(d) determine whether a new position needs to be formed and what it should be responsible for.

The diagnostic system would interact directly with the position software to determine whether a position should be filled by another person to maintain the flow of ideas to implementation and production. This extends the position schema from a structural primitive to a workforce intelligence tool.

## Context

The position schema starts as methodology infrastructure (item 166, Tier 2) — a way to represent roles, understand which position someone is operating from, and ground onboarding documentation. However, the schema itself contains enough structural richness to become a user-facing product.

In this product form, users would:

1. **Define positions**: Structure what a role is responsible for, what decisions it owns, what dependencies it has
2. **Attribute to people**: Assign people to positions, track occupancy history
3. **Discover tools**: The position definition connects to tooling recommendations, SaaS products, and infrastructure needs
4. **Forecast hiring**: The diagnostic system uses position data to flag when capacity breaks down—indicating a position needs another person
5. **Onboard new people**: Generate position-specific documentation based on how the previous occupant operated

This is a downstream product vision (depends on Tier 2 position schema being mature), but worth capturing now.
